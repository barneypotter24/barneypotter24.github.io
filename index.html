Hello, World!

I am currently a Senior at Reed College in Portland, Oregon, majoring in Mathematics and Biology, and I am particularly interested in computational biology and computer science. For my senior thesis, I am implementing Prize-Collecting Stiner Trees in directed signaling hypergraphs, under the advice of Dr. Anna Ritz, and Dr. Jim Fix. I have worked on research questions in transcriptomics, in particular, regarding the effect of whole genome duplication on global gene expression patterns in angiosperm plants.

On the side, I am an avid gamer, and I am learning how to create indie games in my free time. I am particularly interested in RPGs (Role Playing Games) and MOBA (Multiplayer Online Battle Arena) games. My current side project is a text-based RPG, based on the adventures of a lonely skeleton, alone in a world full of dangerous adventurers.

In addition developing games, I am developing my skills as a birder, and trying to expand my life list, starting with the birds of the Pacific Northwest. I also love to raise plants at my home; I am currently trying to convince some passionfruit seeds to grow in Portland.
